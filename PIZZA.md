# Elements of Pizza — 3-Pie scaled recipe (24-Hour Cold Ferment)

Since you are baking tomorrow (Sunday) and it is currently Saturday evening, you should **start the dough tonight** to allow for a 24-hour cold ferment. This recipe scales Ken Forkish's principles (70% hydration, autolyse, minimal yeast, cold fermentation) to yield **three ~290g dough balls** (perfect for 11–12" pizzas).

---

## The Dough (Makes 3 × 290g balls)

| Ingredient | Weight | Baker's % | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Flour** (Bread flour or Typo 00, ~13% protein) | 500g | 100% | High protein yields best gluten structure |
| **Water** (Room temp, ~68°F / 20°C) | 350g | 70% | High hydration, classic Forkish style |
| **Fine Sea Salt** | 12g | 2.4% | Crucial for gluten strength and flavor |
| **Instant Dry Yeast** (IDY) | 0.9g | 0.18% | Just under 1/3 tsp; keeps rise slow and controlled |
| **Extra-Virgin Olive Oil** | 15g | 3% | Helps tenderize the crumb and prevents drying |

> [!NOTE]
> The very low yeast percentage is intentional. It relies on a long, slow cold ferment in the refrigerator to build complex flavors and ensure the dough is highly digestible.

---

## Method

### Saturday Evening (Tonight)

1. **Autolyse**: In a large bowl, mix the flour and water by hand until no dry patches remain. Cover and let rest for **20 to 30 minutes**.
2. **Incorporate Salt & Yeast**: Sprinkle the salt and yeast over the dough. Using wet hands, fold and pinch the dough to fully incorporate them.
3. **Incorporate Oil**: Drizzle in the olive oil and fold the dough over itself until the oil is completely absorbed and the dough is cohesive.
4. **Develop Gluten (Stretch & Fold)**: Perform **3 sets of stretch & folds** spaced 15 minutes apart. Cover between sets.
5. **Bulk Ferment**: Let the dough rest covered at room temperature for **1 to 2 hours** until it shows signs of life (puffy and showing small bubbles).
6. **Divide & Shape**: Dust your work surface. Divide the dough into 3 equal portions (approx. 290g each). Shape each into a tight round ball by tensioning it against the counter.
7. **Cold Ferment**: Place each ball into a lightly oiled container or tray. Cover tightly and refrigerate for **18 to 24 hours**.

---

### Sunday (Bake Day)

* **3 to 4 hours before baking**: Remove the dough containers from the fridge. Let them sit, covered, at room temperature. Cold dough is elastic and will shrink back or tear when stretched.
* **Preheat Oven / Ooni**: Preheating is key. If using a home oven with a pizza stone/steel, preheat at max temp (500°F–550°F / 250°C–280°C) for at least 1 hour. If using an Ooni, heat until the stone reaches **750°F–800°F (400°C–430°C)**, then dial back the flame slightly before launching.

## Pizza Topping Plan (4 Adults & 2 Toddlers)

Toddlers (3-year-olds) typically prefer simple textures, mild flavors, and easily chewable toppings. Adults appreciate complex flavor profiles, balancing savory, sweet, and acidic elements. Here is a curated menu for the 3 pizzas to keep everyone happy:

### 1. The Toddler-First Cheese & Mild Sausage Pizza
*This is a classic crowd-pleaser tailored for toddlers but loved by all.*
* **Base**: Mild tomato sauce (crushed San Marzano tomatoes + pinch of salt).
* **Cheese**: Low-moisture mozzarella (shredded or torn).
* **Toppings**: Fully cooked, mild Italian sausage (crumbled finely so it's easy for toddlers to chew) or simple ham/mild pepperoni.
* **Adult Twist**: Keep a side of hot honey or red pepper flakes to drizzle on adult slices.

### 2. The Transitional Margherita & Mushroom Pizza (Half-and-Half)
*Excellent bridge pizza. One half is kept plain and simple for kids, the other half is elevated.*
* **Kids' Side**: Tomato sauce, mozzarella, and a tiny sprinkle of mild parmesan.
* **Adults' Side**: Add earthy cremini or button mushrooms (sliced thin) and a touch of fresh thyme.
* **Post-Bake**: Drizzle the adults' side with a touch of truffle-infused olive oil or high-quality extra virgin olive oil.

### 3. The Gourmet Adult Specialty (Prosciutto, Rocket & Balsamic)
*Reserved entirely for the adults' sophisticated palates.*
* **Base**: Very light tomato sauce or a white base (drizzle of olive oil, minced garlic, and mozzarella).
* **Post-Bake (Critical)**:
  * Freshly draped prosciutto slices.
  * A handful of fresh, peppery baby arugula (rocket).
  * Shaved Parmigiano-Reggiano.
  * A light drizzle of sweet balsamic glaze or hot honey.
